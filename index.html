Portugal retirement planning

Key facts, tax landscape, and planning dimensions for US advisors preparing clients for retirement in Portugal.

NHR is closed. IFICI (NHR 2.0) does not apply to retirees. The original NHR program ended March 2025. Its replacement requires active employment in qualifying professional sectors. Passive-income retirees are subject to standard Portuguese progressive rates up to 48%. The US–Portugal tax treaty and pre-departure structuring are now the primary planning levers.

Roth IRA in Portugal

Portugal does not recognize US tax-free Roth treatment. Growth is taxable as pension income. For clients with significant Roth positions, this requires explicit re-modeling.

Portugal taxes Roth IRA investment growth as pension income under CIRS Article 54. Original after-tax contributions are exempt (capital reimbursement). The growth component is taxable at progressive rates up to 48%. For a Roth held for 20+ years, growth may represent 70–85% of the account value.
The contribution/growth split
Planning strategies
Distribution calculator
Contributions (basis)
Exempt — CIRS Article 54
After-tax contributions and pre-departure conversion amounts are treated as capital reimbursement — not taxable in Portugal. Requires Form 8606 documentation.
Investment growth
Taxable as foreign pension income
Growth above contributions is classified as Category H pension income and taxed at Portugal's progressive rates (13–48%). Same treatment as traditional IRA, just on a smaller portion.
85/15 fallback rule
If basis cannot be documented
When contribution records are unavailable, Portuguese law allows 85% to be treated as exempt capital reimbursement and only 15% to be taxed. This is a default — documentation can override it.
No official PT guidance
Derived from CIRS by analogy
Portugal has issued no specific ruling on Roth IRAs. Treatment is derived by applying pension/annuity rules. A licensed Portuguese tax attorney is essential for structuring distributions correctly.

Form 8606 is the foundational document. Pull records for every year contributions or conversions were made. Build a cumulative basis ledger. This determines the tax-free portion of every future Portuguese distribution. Do not wait until close to departure — IRS transcripts can be requested but take time. If records are lost, the client defaults to 85/15 regardless of their actual contribution ratio.
Conversions while already a Portuguese resident are an unresolved tax question. Portugal may treat the conversion event itself as a taxable pension distribution — effectively taxing it twice. All conversions must be completed before establishing Portuguese tax residency. Do not convert post-move without specific Portuguese tax counsel guidance.
Pre-departure conversions: still beneficial, but for a different reason
Still recommended
Conversions remain valuable even knowing Portugal taxes the growth component. Each dollar converted pre-departure becomes documented basis — exempt in Portugal. The math: pay US tax on conversion now, then only Portuguese tax on future growth (a smaller fraction). For clients in lower US brackets pre-departure, this almost always still favors converting. The key shift is that conversions reduce the taxable fraction of future distributions rather than creating fully tax-free income.
Form 8606 record-keeping — start immediately
Action required now
For every client with a Roth who might move to Portugal: pull Form 8606 for every year contributions or conversions were made. Build a cumulative basis ledger. This is the documentation that establishes the tax-free portion in Portugal. If records are incomplete, the client defaults to the 85/15 rule — which may or may not be favorable depending on appreciation.
Model 85/15 vs. actual basis each year — it changes annually
Annual decision
As the Roth grows or as distributions reduce the balance, the better method shifts. Calculate both each year: actual contribution ratio vs. 85% fixed. Use whichever produces the lower taxable amount. Establish the method with Portuguese counsel in Year 1 and apply consistently — switching methods creates audit risk.
Sequence contributions drawn first in high-bracket years
Sequencing strategy
Drawing the contribution portion first (zero Portuguese tax) in years when other income pushes brackets high can smooth the overall tax burden. Coordinate with the bucket refill strategy — use Roth contributions to refill Bucket 1 in years when IRA distributions or capital gains have already pushed brackets up.
Position Roth as longevity reserve — not primary income
Asset location shift
The Roth's advantages in Portugal are: no RMDs (flexibility), contributions fully exempt, and ability to grow tax-deferred for later years. Position it as Bucket 3 (longevity reserve) rather than primary income. Draw taxable accounts and traditional IRA distributions first to manage brackets, leaving the Roth for later years when basis represents a larger share of distributions or when other income has declined.
Do NOT convert while a Portuguese tax resident
Hard rule
How Portugal treats a traditional-to-Roth conversion while already resident is unresolved. Portugal may tax the conversion as pension income at progressive rates — on top of US income tax on the same conversion. Until there is clear guidance from the Portuguese tax authority, conversions are a pre-departure-only strategy.

Compliance layer
FBAR (FinCEN 114)
Annual US filing
Required if aggregate foreign accounts exceed $10,000 at any point during the year. Due April 15, auto-extended to Oct 15. Penalties: $10,000+ per violation.
FATCA Form 8938
Filed with US tax return
Threshold: $200k at year-end or $300k at any time (for those abroad). Reports foreign accounts AND assets. Separate from FBAR — both may apply.
Portuguese Modelo 3
Annual PT filing
Worldwide income must be declared. IRA distributions, US dividends, interest all reportable. Foreign taxes paid are creditable. Due June 30 each year.
Transfer documentation
Keep paper trail
Portugal may query large incoming transfers. Maintain records of wire source, account, and income type for each transfer. Important if Portuguese tax authority questions whether funds are taxable in PT.
Lump-sum vs. regular distributions affect Portuguese bracket exposure. A single large IRA withdrawal in one year can spike into the 45–48% bracket. Spreading distributions across years — or pre-departure Roth conversions — can prevent bracket spikes significantly. Model distribution sizing carefully.

Cross-border bucket strategy

The traditional bucket framework rebuilt for Portugal — replacing annuities with US-compliant instruments.

Traditional annuities do not work in this context. US variable annuities held by a Portuguese tax resident create PFIC exposure and unclear Portuguese tax treatment. Portuguese insurance products are inaccessible to US citizens under FATCA. The bucket framework must be rebuilt with compliant instruments only.

EUR-hedged US bond allocation (optional)

Long-term growth

Owned property as the most durable EUR hedge. Paying off Portuguese property outright removes the largest recurring EUR liability from the budget permanently. It eliminates rent inflation risk and FX exposure on housing — the closest functional analog to annuity income certainty, but on the expense side of the ledger rather than the income side.

    Specialist referral guide

    This resource prepares advisors up to the handoff point. These are the specialists every Portugal-retiring client will need — and when to engage each.

    ⚖️
    Portuguese tax attorney / Contabilista Certificado
    Files Modelo 3 returns, advises on treaty application and Roth IRA reporting method (contribution/growth split), handles double-taxation analysis, liaises with Autoridade Tributária. Critical role post-NHR closure.
    Engage before first Portuguese tax year
    🏛️
    Portuguese immigration lawyer (Advogado)
    Handles D7 visa applications, AIMA appointments and follow-up, residency permit renewals, and citizenship pathway planning. AIMA processing times are lengthy — early engagement critical.
    Engage 6–12 months before intended move
    🏠
    Portuguese real estate lawyer (Solicitador)
    Reviews CPCV (Promissory Purchase Contract), conducts title due diligence, handles escritura, confirms no liens or encumbrances. Never use seller's agent's recommended solicitor.
    Engage before signing any property contract
    💼
    US expat CPA / international tax attorney
    Manages US filing obligations (FBAR, Form 8938, Form 8606, Form 1040), PFIC analysis, Foreign Tax Credit optimization, pre-departure Roth conversion strategy, and state tax domicile exit planning.
    Engage 1–2 years before move for pre-departure planning
    🏦
    Fee-only international financial planner
    Cross-border asset allocation, currency risk management, US brokerage account resolution, combined US+PT effective tax rate modeling, and bucket strategy implementation coordination.
    Engage 12–18 months before move
    🏥
    International health insurance broker
    Sources expat health plans meeting D7 visa requirements, coordinates SNS enrollment, advises on Medicare Part B suspension vs. retention decision and re-enrollment penalty implications.
    Engage 3–6 months before move
