Quick take
The job is no longer “find the special regime.” It is “sequence the move correctly, preserve account access, and control which items become Portuguese-taxable.”

              Roth IRA in Portugal

              Portugal does not recognize US Roth tax-free treatment. Growth is generally the portion at risk for Portuguese taxation. For Roth-heavy households, this is a first-rank planning topic.

              Quick take
              A large Roth does not disappear as a planning problem in Portugal. It becomes a basis-documentation and distribution-sequencing problem.
              What matters most
              Pre-move Roth planning, Form 8606 support, and choosing between actual basis and fallback treatment drive the result.
              Common planner mistake
              Assuming “Roth means tax-free” survives cross-border. It does not automatically survive in Portugal.
              Do before move / do after move
              Before move: conversions and records. After move: apply the selected method consistently with PT counsel.
              Portugal taxes Roth IRA investment growth as pension income under the logic commonly derived from CIRS Article 54. Contributions and documented conversion basis are the key exempt portion. This is why large Roth balances should elevate Roth planning inside the intake engine.

              Income distribution mechanics

              How funds move from US accounts to Portugal, where friction enters, and where advisors should intervene before the client becomes operationally dependent on a weak setup.

              Quick take
              The distribution plan is partly a tax issue and partly an operating system issue. Clients feel the failure points here immediately.
              What matters most
              US banking continuity, brokerage permissions, FX efficiency, and a Portuguese cash buffer are the core controls.
              Common planner mistake
              Planning the investment allocation but not the living-money pipe. The client then discovers avoidable wire friction, spread drag, or access restrictions after arrival.
              Do before move / do after move
              Before move: account policy review, platform selection, and transfer testing. After move: maintain discipline on buffer, records, and transfer cadence.
              US side
              Income sources
              SSA, IRA withdrawals, pensions, brokerage cash flow, and sale proceeds all begin in a US framework.
              →
              Bridge layer
              FX and transfer control
              Expat-friendly banking and a specialist FX platform reduce cost and improve reliability.
              →
              Portugal side
              EUR operating system
              A Portuguese account with several months of living cash reduces forced timing and poor conversion decisions.
              Brokerage-heavy households should elevate brokerage restriction review. That logic is now built into the intake module because it is one of the most common advisor misses.

              Cross-border bucket strategy

              The familiar bucket framework adapted for a US client living in Portugal, with emphasis on compliant holdings and sequencing logic rather than product novelty.

              Quick take
              The bucket concept still works, but the product set must stay compliant with US and cross-border realities.
              What matters most
              Short-term EUR liquidity, US-domiciled holdings, and thoughtful use of retirement accounts matter more than product packaging.
              Common planner mistake
              Trying to plug in local non-US funds or annuity-like shortcuts that create PFIC or tax-treatment complications.
              Do before move / do after move
              Before move: confirm what stays US-domiciled. After move: manage refills and annual tax-aware sequencing.
              No casual use of non-US funds. The advisor tool should steer households away from PFIC exposure and toward clean, explainable structures.
              Bucket 1
              Liquidity & living
              Years 1–3
              Funds near-term living without forced liquidation or bad FX timing.
              EUR cash reserve
              USD cash / money market
              T-bills / short duration reserves
              Treaty-protected SS if available
              Bucket 2
              Income generation
              Years 4–12
              Feeds Bucket 1 while controlling bracket spikes and compliance issues.
              US-domiciled bond ETFs
              Traditional IRA distributions sized intentionally
              Roth basis tranches where useful
              Dividend exposure with tax awareness
              Bucket 3
              Long-term growth
              Years 13+
              Long-horizon growth pool that avoids unnecessary cross-border product problems.

                Do before move / do after move
